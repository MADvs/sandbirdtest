How Page Builders Slow Your Site Down

Written by Michael Davis
05-22-2024

Imagine, if you will, that you’ve just ordered new kitchen cabinets from Amazon. You would expect all the parts — The nuts and bolts, the hinges and handles, the wooden pieces, and instructions on how to put everything together — To all arrive in a single box, in one delivery. However, without realizing it, you bought these cabinets not from a company that actually manufactures the cabinets, but a middle-man who places orders for each individual part from separate distributors and has them delivered right to you.


So now, you’re getting a wide array of packages at your front door. One day, a small envelope with the nuts and bolts come along. The next day you get the hinges, and then the handles, and so on. That would be really annoying, wouldn’t it? You’re sitting around, waiting on all these different pieces to arrive before you can even begin to assemble the darned thing.


This is exactly how page builders like Wix, WordPress, or Squarespace work. In this analogy, the parts are your content, the cabinet itself is your website, and you are the web browser. When you build your website using services like these, every change you make is stored in a database that has to be dug around for and accessed every single time someone visits your site. It has to find all the content from your page across a multitude of servers and instructions on how to piece it all together before it gets delivered to the user’s web browser.


Even though this is generally done within seconds, it matters! Research shows that 40-57% of all internet users will abandon a website if it isn’t loaded within three seconds. That’s half of your potential customers gone, not because your services or products aren’t quality, or visitors aren’t interested in them, but simply because your website is too slow.


At Flamewing, we do things differently. We are the amazon delivery you would expect from the cabinet analogy. Nothing on our sites is stored in a database or library that has to be accessed later. We don’t utilize frameworks or pre-built templates. We hand-code every single line of our websites from the ground up, with all the content, instructions on how to piece it together, and everything - all to be delivered to the web browser in one single package.


This approach speeds up load times significantly. We only include what is absolutely necessary to make your site responsive, accessible, and beautiful. Our sites often appear in just under one second, virtually instantaneously. This will ensure that, when a client visits your website, they are far less likely to abandon it and find someone else, which helps bring more revenue to your business!


If you are concerned about potential problems with your existing website, we recommend you run it through this site: pagespeed.web.dev. This is a developer tool created by Google that breaks down where the problems in your site may lie. Websites built with page builders often have very low speed, accessibility, and SEO scores.
